Writing HEX-Programs

The language of HEX-programs is an extension of disjunctive datalog. syntactically, it is strongly related to DLV-syntax with the addition of higher-order atoms and external atoms.

The following list displays the language constructs that correspond to DLV (along with the links to the respective DLV-manual section):

  • Facts (EDB)
  • Rules (IDB)
  • Integrity constraints
  • Weak Constraints
  • Comparative Predicates
  • Aggregate Predicates

Also the chapter about safety there as well applies to dlvhex.

That said, these are the elements of the DLV language that dlvhex does not parse (yet):

  • Arithmetic Predicates
  • Division and Modulo
  • Ranges
  • Queries

Please see also the Getting Started guide.
